Quick Start

There are two options to submit your proof to the Royal Society of Chemistry.

1. Online proofing within Proof Central. This guide provides detailed instructions on how to use the online tools within Proof Central to apply your proof corrections.

2. Annotate PDF. If you would prefer not to use the HTML proofing tool, you can upload an annotated proof PDF of your article to Proof Central.

  • You check the author's names (last names are highlighted teal) are correct - please leave instructions if any changes are required.
  • You review your whole article and make any changes that are required.
  • You answer all the queries. You cannot submit your article without answering all the queries.
  • You provide replacement graphics if needed, or annotate the graphics with instructions for how they should be changed. Only minor changes to the figures (e.g. spelling errors, resizing) should be indicated using annotations. If extensive revisions are required, please attach a new version of the figure.

Interface

  • When you click from the landing page of Proof Central, you are taken to your article, which has been styled to the Royal Society of Chemistry format, presented in a two-pane layout.
  • You can download a typeset PDF proof for your article by clicking the 'PDF' button at the top of the screen. This PDF can be used to check the layout of the article while making your corrections in the HTML interface

(1) Left Pane

  • The left larger pane houses the body of your article, under the Article tab as a default in the edit view. When you select any content on the left pane a floating contextual menu comes up with handles for styling, editing and communication (see details).
  • The left pane supports a ‘Researcher infomation & TOC’ tab under which Proof Central lists the ORCID, ResearcherID and funding details, as well as the contents entry submitted by you.

(2) Right Pane

  • The right pane has four controls. The topmost lets you address copyeditor queries. The second control lets you insert content into the body of the article.
  • The third control lets you leave instructions for your publisher to work on after you submit the proof. This is the best way to ask for changes to the layout of your article or to request a second version of the proof before final publication. The last control tracks and lists all changes during the proof correction process.

Queries

  • The Queries dialog at the top of the right pane is activated by default while loading the article, also activated by clicking on the control. The queries are stacked Q1, Q2, Q3…, click on (query label or query text) any one query to view and act on it using the dialog below the query text. This response dialog has rich text formatting options for you to set your text in bold/italic/superscript/subscript.
  • The queries/insert/instruct dialog box also lets you access special characters that you may wish to insert. A palette of special characters to select from will be presented. Please note you have the option to select special characters not available in the palette through a link to an extensive Unicode table.
  • You can respond to a query with an attachment* and provide remarks on how the attachment should be used by the publisher.

    * Supports all formats other than: sh, asp, cgi, php, php3, ph3, php4, ph4, php5, ph5, phtm, phtml, js, cgi, pl. File size not exceeding 20mb per file.

  • Once you have written your response to a query, click for our servers to register your response. Please note that you must respond to every query before you are able to submit your final corrections. As you respond, the number of pending queries will count down till zero is reached and the query tabs turn green.

Floating Contexual Menu

  • The floating contextual menu comes up when you select/click content in the left pane. It has controls for in situ styling (bold, italic, superscript, subscript).
  • The floating menu provides editing controls - Delete, which operates directly in the text, and shortcuts to the Insert and Instruct controls in the right pane..

Insert

  • To insert new content into your article, open the Insert input box by clicking the Input control available in the floating contexual menu. Key in the necessary content, style it as rich text using the formatting options and click . You must point your cursor to a specific location within the article where the new content should be inserted.
  • Once you do so and click again, the inserted content appears in the chosen place and in green with a yellow background, so you can keep an easy eye on the new content that you introduce.
  • In case you want to undo your insert, click anywhere on the inserted content to bring up the Insert input box, which has a control for clearing the Inserted text.You can also undo any copyediting changes shown in the article in green or red using the Undo control in the floating contextual menu. Please note the available controls in the floating menu vary according to the change type.

Instruct

  • Proof Central lets you pass on an instruction to the Royal Society of Chemistry with changes that you are unable to make yourself.
  • Click on the Instruct control through the floating contextual menu and launch the instruct dialog in the right pane to key in your instruction. You should then click for our servers to register the information. Similar to the queries dialog, the Instruct dialog permits attachment of files (see details).
  • Proof Central allows you to place an instruction on top of an edit. In such cases, please note that when you undo the edit, the instruction that is tethered to the edit will also be undone.

Generic Attachment

  • Proof Central allows you to attach additional files using Attach additional files button. This will invoke Instruct dialog in the right pane, where you submit one or more attachments. Refer to section Queries to find other ways to add attachments.

Math Annotation

    • Proof Central facilitates modifying scientific and technical equations through annotated image instructions for publisher to correct offline.

Edit Log

  • The bottom control on the right panel provides a list of all edits made by the authors.
  • You can click on each list item in the Edit Log to get to the edit location in the left pane.
  • The edit location flashes as you approach it. You may then choose to modify the edit as desired, using the available actions in the floating menu, or the Queries, Insert or Instruct control on the right pane.

Save & Submit

  • When you make a change to the article, the Save button is activated for you to save the changes. Your changes are autosaved too.
  • ProofCentral creates a record of your actions while correcting your proof. You can generate and download this ‘Session Report’ by clicking on this icon.
  • Clicking on Submit sends your corrections to the Royal Society of Chemistry. You will not be able to make any further changes to your article after clicking submit. A consolidated Session Report is auto-generated and mailed to you after submission of your corrections.
